## 1. Project overview

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Full title** | Accelerated Variety Turnover for Open-Pollinated Crops in Tanzania (**ACCELERATE**) |
| **Lead** | Alliance of Bioversity International & CIAT (ABC) / Pan-Africa Bean Research Alliance (PABRA) |
| **Funder** | Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation (BMGF) |
| **Duration** | 2023–2026 (four-year project) |
| **Country** | Tanzania |
| **Target crops** | Common bean, sorghum, groundnut |

**Goal:** Create a *scalable model that fosters the rapid adoption of new OPV (open-pollinated
variety) crop varieties and accelerates varietal turnover in Tanzania* — then make it replicable
for other regions and value chains.

**Problem addressed:** Adoption of OPVs remains low due to lack of product information, limited
promotion, inadequate access to early-generation seed, insufficient data to guide adoption, and a
weak seed supply system. Only **~3% of farmers' planting requirements** are met by the formal /
semi-formal seed sector — the rest comes from informal sources, perpetuating use of old,
low-yielding, climate-vulnerable varieties.

**Strategic approach (demand-pull / demand-led seed system):** Link the capacities of formal,
semi-formal, and informal seed sectors with existing sources of demand — positioning **grain
traders, aggregators, and processors as focal points** that drive demand for quality seed.

### Three working hypotheses
1. Increasing information flow to and from large traders, grain producers, and formal/semi-formal
   seed producers will **drive demand pull** for quality seed.
2. Enhanced engagement with **marketplace traders** boosts adoption through demand-pull.
3. **Institutional buyer** awareness and access accelerate varietal turnover, improving farmer
   income and nutrition outcomes.

## 3. Value-chain actors (companies / traders)

> These are real ACCELERATE partner enterprises — high-value seed records for the actor registry.

### Bora Food Company Ltd
- **Role:** Lead private partner (processor/aggregator); bio-fortified varieties for school nutrition.
- **Location:** Rwenzewe Ward, Bukombe District, Geita Region.
- **Crops/capacity (annual processing):** 180 MT beans, 18 MT groundnuts, 60 MT sorghum.
- **Engagement:** Trained 20 QDS farmers (13 men, 7 women, May 2024, 3 acres each); each contributed
  TZS 50,000 (~USD 19). Seed starter packs: 70 kg Uyole 16 + 20 kg TARI Bean 6. Works with 200+
  farmers via informal agreements; target ~300 farmers next season.
- **Markets:** Dodoma, Mwanza, Dar es Salaam.

### Ikuwo (IKUWO) General Enterprises Limited
- **Established:** 1995. **Role:** Bean value chain — input supply, grain trading, QDS production & distribution.
- **Location:** Sumbawanga, Rukwa Region (southern highlands); also operates in Katavi Region.
- **Bean varieties:** Uyole Njano, Calima Uyole, Uyole 03, JESCA, Uyole 18.
- **Model:** Foundation seed from TARI → 40 QDS producers → 180 agro-dealers (5/25/100 kg packs);
  3 warehouses as aggregation centers; farmer training, demo plots, radio programs.
- **Scale:** 2023 target 56 MT QDS; trade volume grew 1,000 MT (2017) → 5,000 MT (2022);
  benefits ~12,000 farmers across 40 contracted groups; yields up 0.3 → 0.8 t/acre; USD 5.4M invested.
- **Exports:** DRC, Zambia (700 MT), Kenya, Burundi, Uganda.
- **Partners:** PABRA, TARI, CRDB Bank, NEXTBYTES (SMS climate info), Ndingala FM (radio).

### Kibaigwa Flour Supplies Ltd (KFS)
- **Managing Director:** Sebastian Msola. **Role:** Sorghum/groundnut processor; fortified flours.
- **Location:** Kongwa District, Dodoma Region.
- **Sorghum varieties:** TARI SOR 1 (red), TARI SOR 2 (white) — both 3–4 t/ha, Striga & bird resistant.
- **Groundnut varieties:** NARINUT 2015 (tan, 1.2–2 t/ha, rosette resistant), NALIENDELE 2016 (red, 1–2 t/ha, rosette resistant).
- **Model:** Contract farming — seed on credit, GAP training, guaranteed purchase (paid TZS 550/kg
  sorghum vs competitors' 480/kg in 2022/23).
- **Reach:** 7,850 farmers supported (3,400 contracted). 16 demo plots (11 sorghum, 5 groundnut)
  across Kongwa & Chamwino. Field day 17 May 2024 (105 attendees); QDS training 20–21 June 2024
  (20 farmers + 3 traders). Ordered 300 kg TARI SOR 1/2.
- **Markets:** Tanzania + Rwanda, Burundi, Kenya.
- **Partners:** ABC, TARI, TOSCI, CIMMYT, A-Z Company/AgroZ (aflatoxin), NMB Bank, East African Grain Council (EAGC).

### Ntemisambo Company Limited
- **Established:** 2019. **Director:** Sambo Luhende. **Role:** Cereal trading & processing (rice, sunflower, groundnut).
- **Location:** Mpanda District, Katavi Region.
- **Groundnut varieties:** Naliendele 2016, Tanzanut 2016, Nachi 2015, Narinut 2015.
- **Scale:** Sourced 15 MT groundnuts (2023/24) → 113.5 MT (2024/25); 510 kg seed for demo/multiplication;
  25.5 acres seed production; 14 contracted seed growers; 5-year target 5,000 MT.
- **Engagement:** Trained 20 farmers (5 women, 2 extension officers) in QDS.
- **Markets:** Mpanda, Tabora, Rukwa.
- **Partners:** TARI Naliendele, ALSEM Seed Company, DASPA, Agriculture Council of Tanzania (ACT), ACCELERATE.

### Seasoning Palet
- **Role:** Groundnut value-addition (peanut butter, snacks) using improved varieties.

## 6. Key statistics

- Formal seed sector meets **~3%** of farmers' planting needs.
- **50+** stakeholders at the Year 1 annual planning meeting (Arusha).
- Bean yields: ~0.78 t/acre (10 yrs ago) → 1.3 t/acre today → target 2 t/acre.
- Market intelligence workshops: Arusha, Dodoma, Dar es Salaam, **21–25 Oct 2024**.

### Market intelligence findings (CIMMYT)
- Rising demand for groundnut, sorghum, beans (population growth + export markets).
- Export markets need **pure white sorghum** and pest-free beans; processors need
  nutrient-enhanced, product-specific varieties (peanut butter, flour).
- Barriers: inconsistent quality supply, certified-seed access, weak distribution to remote areas,
  poor pre/post-harvest handling, **aflatoxin** contamination, consumer misconceptions
  (e.g. white sorghum browning during processing).
- Recommendations: scale QDS, expand distribution, capacity-building on handling, align breeding
  with market needs, strengthen private-seed partnerships, deploy digital tools (WhatsApp, SMS).

### Digital / climate-info findings (Frontiers in Sustainable Food Systems, 2025)
- *"Assessing climate events, farmer adaptation, and the role of social media in climate and
  varietal information delivery among Tanzanian farmers"* — Kalemera, Venkataramana, Mbega,
  Assefa, Ijumulana, Ochieng, Rubyogo.
- 315 households, 4 regions (Siha, Karatu, Mbozi, Sumbawanga); 2 yrs WhatsApp + 30 yrs NASA POWER data.
- 18.4% use social media; 22% own smartphones; WhatsApp = 67% of usage; only 10% access
  "which variety to plant" info; best engagement 19:00–20:00 and off-season; 68% trust the info.
